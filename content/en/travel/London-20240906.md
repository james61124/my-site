### **The Phantom Of The Opera**

![The-Phantom-Of-The-Opera-2](/images/travel/London-20240906/The-Phantom-Of-The-Opera-2.jpg)

The theater itself isn‚Äôt very large, but sitting closer lets you clearly see the iconic chandelier fall scene. Otherwise, parts of it might be blocked by the ceiling.

![The-Phantom-Of-The-Opera](/images/travel/London-20240906/The-Phantom-Of-The-Opera.jpg)

There‚Äôs so much I want to say, but it‚Äôs hard to express in words. A great musical surprises you with its script, stage design, and music.

The harmonies were especially striking ‚Äì many parts were dissonant, reflecting the suspense and anger in the plot. It reminded me of random, chaotic sounds I‚Äôd make by slamming piano keys, yet it perfectly conveyed the eerie atmosphere. The composers are truly incredible.

Regarding stage design ‚Äì even though I couldn‚Äôt fully understand the dialogue, many of the visual elements on stage were eye-catching. For example, in Act 2, when the male lead is assassinated and replaced by the Phantom, the chaos is portrayed by rushing a stretcher with a covered body across the stage. Another scene that stood out to me was the contrast between the happy couple and the Phantom‚Äôs pain. My favorite part was near the end when the female lead kisses the Phantom ‚Äì not out of love, but to save the male lead. ### **Overnight Experience at STN Airport**

![STN](/images/travel/London-20240906/STN.jpg)
